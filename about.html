Wuruwuru

Wuruwuru is an independent publication about design and internet culture in Nigeria.

The name comes from the phrase wuruwuru to the answer, a term used to describe an inexplicable solution to a problem. My approach to publishing is similarly haphazard.

I'm fascinated by a lot of the content I read and watch e.g. Aeon stories, The Pudding essays, Adult Swim shorts, Vimeo staff picks, magazines like Offscreen and Apartamento, to name a few. Wuruwuru is my attempt to make high quality media like they do.

I don't intend to build a company, so each story will be made in collaboration with independent writers, designers, illustrators, animators and others as needed. For me, this is an opportunity to make fun things with peers; an adult playground, to put it plainly.

I'm taking it one story at a time, each one an exploration of an obscure but fascinating topic. The plan is to make all sorts of things, from interactive essays and animated videos to posters and magazines.

There's no pressure to make a lot of stories and there's no established cadence for publishing.

Wuruwuru is slow, random press.
